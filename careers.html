What after 12th?

Career roadmaps for Indian students

Science, Commerce, or Humanities — map entrances, degrees, and realistic salary bands (indicative; varies by city, college, and experience).

Science (PCM / PCB)

JEE · NEET · CUET Science — India’s largest intake route.

  1. 1
    Class 11–12

    Board exams + coaching for JEE or NEET; strengthen NCERT base.

  2. 2
    Entrance

    PCM → JEE Main & Advanced; PCB → NEET UG; IISER/IISc via additional channels.

  3. 3
    Top degrees

    B.Tech from IIT/NIT; MBBS from AIIMS/state colleges; B.Sc Research at IISc.

  4. 4
    Salary outlook (early career, India)

    Engineering fresher: often ₹6–18 LPA depending on campus & role; MBBS interns/residents: stipend-based until PG.

Commerce

CA, finance, economics, and B-school pipelines.

  1. 1
    Class 11–12

    Accountancy, Economics, Business Studies + maths optional for analytics paths.

  2. 2
    Entrance

    CUET for DU & central universities; IPMAT for IIM Indore/Rohtam integrated MBA; separate forms for private BBA.

  3. 3
    Top degrees

    B.Com (Hons) SRCC; BMS; Integrated MBA (IIMs); CA Foundation parallel track.

  4. 4
    Salary outlook

    UG placements (tier-1 commerce): often ₹8–15 LPA; CA articleship stipend then significant jump post-qualification.

Humanities & Social Sciences

Civil services, law, media, design, and international studies.

  1. 1
    Class 11–12

    History, Polity, Psychology, Literature — build reading & writing depth.

  2. 2
    Entrance

    CUET UG; CLAT for NLUs; separate tests for design (NID/NIFT) and mass comm institutes.

  3. 3
    Top degrees

    BA programmes at JNU, Ashoka, TISS; BA LLB at NLSIU; B.Des at NID.

  4. 4
    Salary outlook

    Varies widely: NGOs & research roles may start modest; law & policy consulting can scale quickly with tier-1 degrees.
